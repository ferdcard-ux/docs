Google gives you a range of controls to decide what data is collected, how long it's kept, and who can see it. Understanding these controls — and knowing where to find them — puts you in charge of your digital footprint. This guide covers everything from browsing your activity history to downloading a full copy of your data.

---

## What Data Google Collects During Search

When you search on Google, the following information may be associated with your account or your device:

- **Search queries** — the exact text or voice input you submit
- **Results you click** — which links you select and when
- **Location signals** — your IP address and, if permitted, precise GPS location
- **Device and browser info** — browser type, operating system, language settings
- **Cookies and session data** — used to personalize results and measure engagement

<Note>
  If you are signed out of your Google account, activity is associated with a browser cookie rather than your personal profile. Clearing cookies removes this association from your device, but Google's server-side logs are separate.
</Note>

## My Activity: Viewing and Deleting Your Search History

My Activity is a chronological log of everything Google has recorded across Search, Maps, YouTube, and other services.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open My Activity">
    Go to [myactivity.google.com](https://myactivity.google.com) or navigate to **Settings → Privacy & Security → My Activity**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter by product">
    Click **Filter by date & product**, select **Search**, and choose a date range to narrow the view.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Delete individual items">
    Hover over any item and click the **✕** icon to delete that single entry. The item is removed immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Delete a range or all history">
    Click **Delete** in the left sidebar and choose:
    - **Last hour**
    - **Last day**
    - **All time**
    - **Custom range** — specify exact start and end dates

    Confirm the deletion in the dialog. Deletion is permanent and cannot be undone.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Deleting your search history affects personalization. Search results, recommendations, and autocomplete suggestions may become less relevant until Google re-learns your preferences.
</Warning>

## Auto-Delete: Automatic History Cleanup

Instead of manually pruning your history, you can instruct Google to automatically delete activity older than a set threshold.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open auto-delete settings">
    Go to **My Activity → Auto-delete** or navigate directly to **Settings → Privacy & Security → History settings → Auto-delete**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a retention period">
    Select one of the available options:

    | Retention period | Best for |
    |-----------------|----------|
    | **3 months** | Maximum privacy; minimal personalization |
    | **18 months** | Balanced privacy and relevance (recommended) |
    | **36 months** | Strong personalization; longer history retained |
    | **Keep until I delete manually** | Full control; no automatic deletion |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Next**, review the confirmation (which shows how much existing history will be deleted immediately), then click **Confirm**. Auto-delete runs continuously from this point forward.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Incognito / Private Mode

<Accordion title="What Incognito mode does">
  When you open an Incognito window in Chrome (or a Private window in other browsers) and search on Google:

  - Your search queries are **not saved** to your Google account history
  - Browsing history is **not stored** in the browser's local history
  - Cookies and site data are **deleted** when you close the window
  - Your employer, school, or ISP **can still see** your network traffic
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What Incognito mode does NOT do">
  Incognito mode is not full anonymity. The following still occur:

  - Your **IP address** is visible to websites and Google's servers
  - Your **ISP** can see which sites you visit
  - If you **sign in to Google** during an Incognito session, your searches are linked to your account
  - **Downloads** you save to disk persist after the window closes
  - **Bookmarks** you create persist after the window closes

  <Warning>
    Do not rely on Incognito mode to hide activity from your network administrator, employer, or law enforcement. Use a reputable VPN or Tor Browser if anonymity at the network level is required.
  </Warning>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Enabling Incognito in the Google mobile app">
  1. Tap your **profile picture** in the top-right corner of the Google app.
  2. Tap **Turn on Incognito**.
  3. A dark theme confirms you are in Incognito mode. Tap your profile picture again and select **Turn off Incognito** to return to your normal session.
</Accordion>

## Location Data

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="How location is used">
    Google uses your location to:
    - Return locally relevant search results (restaurants, weather, stores)
    - Improve Maps and navigation accuracy
    - Personalize ads based on your city or region
    - Power features like "near me" searches

    Location data may come from your IP address, device GPS, Wi-Fi networks, or Bluetooth beacons depending on the permissions you have granted.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Disabling location access">
    **On desktop:**
    1. In Chrome, click the **lock icon** in the address bar on google.com.
    2. Select **Site settings → Location → Block**.

    **On Android:**
    1. Go to **System Settings → Apps → Google → Permissions → Location**.
    2. Select **Deny** or **Allow only while using the app** (the latter is recommended if you use Google Maps).

    **On iOS:**
    1. Go to **Settings → Privacy & Security → Location Services → Google**.
    2. Select **Never** or **While Using the App**.

    <Note>
      Blocking precise location means Google falls back to your IP address for rough geographic context. Some features like "open now" or real-time traffic may be less accurate.
    </Note>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Deleting location history">
    1. Go to [myactivity.google.com](https://myactivity.google.com).
    2. Click **Filter by date & product → Maps** (location history is stored under Maps activity).
    3. Delete individual entries or use **Delete → All time** to clear everything.

    You can also go to **Timeline** in Google Maps → **⚙ Settings → Delete all Location History**.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Downloading Your Data with Google Takeout

Google Takeout lets you export a copy of everything Google knows about your account — search history, emails, photos, contacts, and more.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Google Takeout">
    Navigate to [takeout.google.com](https://takeout.google.com) or go to **Settings → Privacy & Security → Download your data**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select data to export">
    By default, all products are selected. Click **Deselect all**, then check only the products you want — for example, **Search** and **My Activity** for a focused export.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose export options">
    - **File type:** `.zip` (default) or `.tgz`
    - **File size:** Split into 1 GB, 2 GB, 4 GB, 10 GB, or 50 GB chunks
    - **Delivery method:** Email download link, save to Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive, or Box
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create export">
    Click **Create export**. Google prepares your archive in the background — this can take minutes to hours depending on how much data you have. You receive an email when the download is ready.

    <Tip>
      Exports are available to download for 7 days. If you miss the window, you need to initiate a new export.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
</Steps>
