Google Alerts watches the web for you — the moment new content matches a keyword or topic you care about, Google sends it straight to your inbox. Beyond Alerts, you can control how the Google mobile app notifies you and fine-tune exactly which types of messages you receive. This guide walks you through every notification control available to you.

---

## Google Alerts

### Creating an alert

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Google Alerts">
    Navigate to [google.com/alerts](https://google.com/alerts) or open **Settings → Notifications → Alerts** in your account panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter a keyword or topic">
    Type your search term in the **Create an alert about…** field. You can use the same operators you use in Google Search — for example, `"climate change" site:reuters.com` or `electric vehicles -Tesla`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure alert options">
    Click **Show options** to expand the following controls:

    | Option | Description |
    |--------|-------------|
    | **How often** | Real-time, At most once a day, At most once a week |
    | **Sources** | Automatic, News, Blogs, Web, Video, Books, Discussions, Finance |
    | **Language** | Limit results to a specific language |
    | **Region** | Limit results to a specific country |
    | **How many** | Only the best results, or all results |
    | **Deliver to** | Your Google account email, or an RSS feed |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create the alert">
    Click **Create Alert**. Your new alert appears in the **My Alerts** list immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Wrap multi-word phrases in quotes (e.g., `"machine learning"`) to alert on the exact phrase rather than on each word independently. This dramatically reduces noise.
</Tip>

## Email Digest Frequency

You can set each alert to deliver emails at a cadence that suits you.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Real-time">
    Google sends an email as soon as a matching result is indexed. Best for breaking news, brand monitoring, or urgent topics.

    **To set:** Open an alert → **Edit** (pencil icon) → **How often** → **As-it-happens** → **Save**.

    <Warning>
      Real-time alerts on broad keywords (e.g., `AI`) can generate dozens of emails per hour. Use narrow, specific queries with this setting.
    </Warning>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Daily digest">
    Google batches all matching results from the past 24 hours into one email, delivered each morning.

    **To set:** Open an alert → **Edit** → **How often** → **At most once a day** → **Save**.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Weekly digest">
    Google sends one email per week summarizing the top matching results from the past seven days. Ideal for low-urgency topics or research monitoring.

    **To set:** Open an alert → **Edit** → **How often** → **At most once a week** → **Save**.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Managing and Deleting Alerts

<Accordion title="Edit an existing alert">
  1. Go to [google.com/alerts](https://google.com/alerts).
  2. Find the alert in **My Alerts** and click the **pencil icon** on the right.
  3. Adjust any option — keyword, frequency, sources, or delivery address.
  4. Click **Update Alert** to save your changes.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Pause an alert">
  If you want to stop an alert temporarily without deleting it, click the **bell icon** next to the alert to mute it. Click the icon again to resume delivery.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Delete an alert">
  1. Go to [google.com/alerts](https://google.com/alerts).
  2. Click the **trash icon** next to the alert you want to remove.
  3. The alert is deleted immediately — there is no confirmation step and no undo.

  <Note>
    You can also unsubscribe from a specific alert by clicking the **Unsubscribe** link at the bottom of any alert email. This deletes only that alert, not your entire account.
  </Note>
</Accordion>

## Push Notifications (Mobile App)

The Google app for iOS and Android can send push notifications for trending topics, your saved searches, and personalized news.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open app notification settings">
    In the Google app, tap your **profile picture** → **Settings** → **Notifications**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable or disable notification categories">
    Toggle individual categories on or off:

    - **Your alerts** — push versions of your Google Alerts
    - **Trending searches** — topics trending near you right now
    - **News for you** — personalized news based on your interests
    - **Security alerts** — sign-in attempts and account activity warnings
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set quiet hours">
    Tap **Quiet hours** to define a time window (e.g., 10 PM – 7 AM) during which no push notifications are delivered, regardless of category settings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  On iOS, you can also manage Google app notification permissions at the system level under **Settings → Notifications → Google**. System-level settings override in-app settings.
</Tip>

## Stopping a Specific Notification Type

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Unsubscribe via email" icon="envelope-open">
    Every notification email from Google includes an **Unsubscribe** or **Manage notifications** link in the footer. Click it to go directly to the settings for that notification type.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Turn off in Settings" icon="toggle-off">
    Go to **Settings → Notifications**, find the notification category, and toggle it **Off**. Changes take effect within a few minutes.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Block at inbox level" icon="filter">
    In Gmail, open any unwanted Google notification, click **⋮ More** → **Filter messages like this** → **Delete it** to auto-delete future messages of that type without visiting Google Settings.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Revoke app permissions" icon="shield-xmark">
    To stop push notifications entirely for the mobile app, go to your device's system settings and revoke notification permission for the Google app.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
