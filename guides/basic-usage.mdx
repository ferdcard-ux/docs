Google Search is your gateway to the web — type a question, a topic, or a few keywords into the search bar and Google surfaces the most relevant pages, images, videos, and facts in seconds. This guide walks you through everything you need to get started, from your very first query to saving and sharing results with others.

## Performing your first search

Getting started with Google takes only a few seconds. Follow the steps below to run your first query.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Google">
    Navigate to [google.com](https://www.google.com) in any modern web browser. The search bar sits at the center of the page, ready for your input.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Type your query">
    Click inside the search bar and type your question or keywords. You don't need to phrase it as a complete sentence — short, descriptive terms often work best (e.g., `best hiking trails near Seattle`).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit the search">
    Press **Enter** or click the **Google Search** button. Your results page loads within milliseconds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Not sure what to search for? Click **I'm Feeling Lucky** to jump directly to the top result for your query without viewing the full results page.
</Tip>

## Reading and navigating results

The search results page (SERP) is organized to help you find the right answer fast. Understanding its layout makes you a more effective searcher.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Featured Snippet" icon="star" href="/concepts/results">
    A highlighted box at the top of the page that directly answers your query — often pulled from a trusted source.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Organic Results" icon="list" href="/concepts/results">
    The ranked list of web pages most relevant to your query. Each result shows a title, URL, and a brief description.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Knowledge Panel" icon="circle-info" href="/concepts/results">
    A sidebar card (on desktop) that summarizes facts about people, places, or things from sources like Wikipedia.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Related Searches" icon="magnifying-glass" href="/concepts/search">
    Suggestions at the bottom of the page that help you refine or expand your query.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Use the tabs beneath the search bar — **All**, **Images**, **Videos**, **News**, **Maps**, and **Shopping** — to filter results by content type.

## Clicking through to sources

Every organic result links to an external web page. Here's how to navigate them effectively.

- **Left-click** a result title to open the page in your current tab.
- **Middle-click** (or **Ctrl + click** / **Cmd + click**) to open the page in a new tab, keeping your results page intact.
- Click the **three-dot menu** (⋮) next to any result to see options like *About this result*, which tells you more about the source's credibility and your connection to it.

<Note>
  The URL shown beneath a result title is the page's canonical address. You can hover over the link before clicking to preview it in your browser's status bar.
</Note>

## Using search bar features

Google's search bar does much more than accept typed text. Take advantage of these built-in tools to search faster and in new ways.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Autocomplete">
    As you type, Google predicts your query based on popular searches and your own history. Use the **arrow keys** to navigate suggestions and press **Enter** to select one. To dismiss suggestions, press **Escape**.

    ```text title="Example autocomplete flow"
    Typed:     "climate ch"
    Suggested: "climate change causes"
                "climate change solutions"
                "climate change news"
    ```
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Voice Search">
    Click the **microphone icon** inside the search bar (or press the dedicated mic button on mobile) and speak your query aloud. Google transcribes your speech and runs the search automatically.

    <Tip>
      Voice search works best in a quiet environment. Speak naturally — you can say "What's the weather like in Tokyo this weekend?" as a full question.
    </Tip>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Image Search">
    Click the **camera icon** inside the search bar to search by image. You can:

    - **Upload an image** from your device.
    - **Paste an image URL** from the web.
    - **Drag and drop** an image file directly onto the search bar.

    Google identifies objects, landmarks, products, and more within the image and returns visually or contextually related results.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Saving and sharing results

Once you've found what you're looking for, Google gives you several ways to save and share it.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Copy a result link">
    Right-click any result title and choose **Copy link address** to grab the URL. Paste it anywhere — a document, email, or chat.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share directly">
    On mobile, tap the **Share** icon on a result card to send it via messages, email, or any installed app. On desktop, use your browser's share button or simply copy the result URL from the address bar after clicking through.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save to Collections (signed in)">
    When you're signed in to your Google account, click the **bookmark icon** on a result to save it to a Collection. Access your saved items any time at [google.com/save](https://www.google.com/save).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export search results">
    For research workflows, copy the URL of your search results page — it encodes your exact query — and share it so colleagues see the same results. The URL format looks like:

    ```text
    https://www.google.com/search?q=your+query+here
    ```
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Search result pages are dynamic — the same URL may return different results over time as Google's index updates. If you need a permanent record, save individual page URLs rather than the search results URL.
</Warning>
